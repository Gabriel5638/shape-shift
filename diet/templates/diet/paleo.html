Introduction to the Paleo diet

Price: Free

The paleo diet is a nutritional approach that focuses on whole, unprocessed foods. It primarily consists of lean meats, fish, vegetables, fruits, nuts, and seeds. This diet is inspired by the eating habits of our prehistoric ancestors and aims to eliminate processed foods, grains, dairy, and added sugars. By embracing the paleo way of eating, you prioritize natural, nutrient-rich foods, promoting better health and well-being.

Below is a simple recipe to get you started:

Paleo Stuffed Bell Peppers with Tomato Sauce: Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers (red, green, or yellow)
  • 1 pound ground beef or turkey
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup cauliflower rice (finely chopped cauliflower)
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for cooking
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish (optional)
Instructions:
  • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  • Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Rinse them thoroughly.
  • Heat a skillet over medium heat, add a bit of olive oil, and sauté the chopped onion and garlic until they become translucent.
  • Add the ground beef or turkey to the skillet and cook until browned. Break it up into small pieces as it cooks.
  • Stir in the cauliflower rice, diced tomatoes, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few minutes until everything is well combined.
  • Carefully stuff each bell pepper with the mixture.
  • Place the stuffed peppers in a baking dish and pour the tomato sauce from the can into the bottom of the dish.
  • Cover the dish with foil and bake for about 35-40 minutes or until the bell peppers are tender.
  • Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes to allow the tops to brown slightly.
  • Once done, remove from the oven, garnish with fresh basil leaves if desired, and serve hot.
